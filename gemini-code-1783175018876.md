# Typography Guide: Dental Store Website

Choosing the right typography for a dental store is crucial. The fonts should convey **trust, hygiene, professionalism, and approachability**. 

Here are the best font pairings curated for different sections of your platform.

---

## 1. Landing Page & Home Page (Customer Facing)
Your customer-facing pages need to look welcoming and modern while maintaining a medical/professional standard.

### Option A: The "Modern Clinic" (Recommended)
* **Headings (H1, H2, H3):** `Poppins`
  * *Why:* Poppins is a geometric sans-serif that is round and friendly, reducing the "clinical anxiety" often associated with dental services.
* **Body Text (Paragraphs, descriptions):** `Inter` or `Roboto`
  * *Why:* Highly legible, clean, and modern. Perfect for reading product descriptions and clinical details.

### Option B: The "Premium Care"
* **Headings (H1, H2, H3):** `Lora` (Serif)
  * *Why:* Lora is a contemporary serif that adds a touch of elegance, premium quality, and established trust. Great for high-end dental equipment or luxury oral care.
* **Body Text:** `Open Sans`
  * *Why:* Neutral and friendly, it balances the elegance of the serif heading perfectly.

---

## 2. Product Pages & Storefront
When displaying dental tools, toothpaste, or equipment, clarity is key.

* **Product Titles:** `Montserrat` (Bold or Semi-Bold)
* **Prices & SKU numbers:** `Roboto Mono` or `Inter` (Numbers look crisp and aligned)
* **Product Descriptions:** `Inter` (Regular, 16px for readability)
* **Buttons (Add to Cart / Buy Now):** `Montserrat` (Bold, UPPERCASE or Title Case)

---

## 3. Admin Page / Dashboard
The admin dashboard is a functional workspace. You want high data density, readability on small screens, and neutral aesthetics.

* **Dashboard Headings & UI Elements:** `Inter` or `Segoe UI / San Francisco` (System Fonts)
  * *Why:* System fonts load instantly and developers are used to reading them in dense interfaces. Inter was literally designed for computer screens.
* **Data Tables, Invoices, SKUs, and Code:** `JetBrains Mono` or `Fira Code`
  * *Why:* Monospaced fonts ensure that columns of numbers (like inventory counts and prices) align perfectly vertically, preventing reading errors.

---

## 4. Hierarchy & Sizing Guide (Example using Option A)

| Element | Font Family | Weight | Size (Desktop) | Size (Mobile) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main Hero Title (H1)** | Poppins | Bold (700) | 48px | 32px |
| **Section Titles (H2)** | Poppins | SemiBold (600)| 36px | 28px |
| **Sub-headings (H3)** | Poppins | Medium (500) | 24px | 20px |
| **Body Text** | Inter | Regular (400) | 16px - 18px | 16px |
| **Small Text / Captions** | Inter | Regular (400) | 14px | 14px |
| **Buttons / CTA** | Poppins | SemiBold (600)| 16px | 16px |

---

## 5. How to Embed (Google Fonts)
To use the recommended **Poppins + Inter** combination, add this to your website's `<head>`:

<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com">
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.gstatic.com" crossorigin>
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Inter:wght@400;500;600&family=Poppins:wght@500;600;700&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
